I — THE PROBLEM

Ideas die in translation.

Our world is made up of ideas that were communicated well. For all of history that meant human to human. Now it is human to agents — and agents can build almost anything, for almost no one.

Because the bottleneck moved. It is no longer can it be built. It is can you say it so the machine keeps building the right thing after you stop talking. Say it badly and your idea arrives wrong, or arrives expensive, or quietly becomes someone else's idea with your name on it.

II — THE INTERVIEW

One conversation. No specifications.

You never write requirements. You talk — ramble, even. Interrex asks the questions that matter and never asks you to be technical:

THE RAMBLE

Describe the idea in whatever words come. Your phrasing is kept verbatim — the machine learns to speak your language, not the reverse.

THIS OR THAT

No "what are your requirements." Instead: which of these two is more your idea, and what's wrong with the loser? Judgment is easy. Specification was always the hard part — so it's no longer your job.

YOUR RULINGS

You judge a set of concrete scenarios your project might face. Your verdicts become an exam — and no agent gains autonomy over your idea until it rules the way you ruled.

THE FOUNDING

Out the other side: a constitution in your own words, a senate of agents from deliberately different model families, and a repository that starts building. With proof — a score — that your idea was actually understood.

III — THE LAW

Then it runs. As far as you allow.

Every change your project faces is deliberated by the senate against your constitution — debated, voted, recorded, public. You check in as the visionary, as often or as rarely as you like. Ping me at every fork. Or spend the budget and don't contact me until it's done. Your call — and once made, it holds:

An explicit no-contact instruction is binding: it holds even when the senate believes the project is failing. — CONSTITUTION OF INTERREX, ART. II §3

But no setting, however permissive, lowers the floor. Nothing illegal. Nothing harmful. Nothing that puts you at risk to reach your own goal. And your interview is a confidence — your idea is never reused, never published, never marketing:

An interview is a confidence. Broad consent at signup does not satisfy this Article. — THE INTERVIEW SEAL, ART. VIII
